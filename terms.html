1. What Amana is

Amana is a marketplace where people in Katsina buy and sell items directly with each other. Amana is not the seller of any item listed — it provides the platform, reviews listings before they go live, holds buyer payments in escrow, and releases funds to sellers once a purchase is confirmed.

By listing an item, placing an order, or otherwise using Amana, you agree to these terms.

2. Terms for buyers

When you buy an item on Amana:

  • You pay the exact price the seller listed. Nothing is added at checkout.
  • Your payment is held by Amana, not released to the seller, until you confirm the item arrived as described.
  • You're responsible for arranging the handoff or delivery directly with the seller once your order is confirmed — Amana is not a courier or delivery service.
  • If an item doesn't match its listing, you can raise a dispute (see Section 5) instead of confirming.

3. Terms for sellers

When you list and sell an item on Amana:

  • Every listing is reviewed by Amana before it goes live — usually within a day.
  • You're responsible for the accuracy of your listing: description, condition, photos, and price.
  • You're responsible for arranging handoff or delivery directly with the buyer once an order is placed.
  • Payment is held in escrow and released to you only after the buyer confirms the item arrived — see Sections 4 and 5.
  • Repeated inaccurate listings, no-shows, or disputes found in your favor to be unfounded may result in your seller account being suspended.

4. Fees & payouts

Amana charges a platform fee of 5% on every completed marketplace sale.

This fee is deducted from the seller's payout, not added to what the buyer pays. If you list an item at ₦100,000, the buyer pays ₦100,000, and you receive ₦95,000 once the sale is confirmed and released.

By submitting a listing, a seller agrees to this fee being deducted from their payout automatically — no separate action is needed at the time of sale.

5. Escrow, disputes & refunds

Amana holds every marketplace payment until the transaction is confirmed complete:

  • Once a buyer marks an order as arrived, the seller's payout is automatically released after a 48-hour window, unless the order is disputed in that window.
  • A buyer can raise a dispute on an order once it's marked "arrived," if the item doesn't match what was ordered.
  • Disputed orders are reviewed by Amana, and payout is either released to the seller or refunded to the buyer based on that review.
  • Once a payout has been released or a refund issued, that decision is final.

6. Listing rules

  • Listings must be for real items the seller currently has in their possession or can fulfill as described.
  • Photos must be of the actual item being sold, not stock or placeholder images.
  • Prohibited, counterfeit, or illegal items are not permitted and will be rejected during review.
  • Amana reserves the right to reject or remove any listing that doesn't meet these standards.

7. Accounts & verification

  • Buying, selling, and saving items require a verified email address, confirmed by a one-time code.
  • Selling additionally requires a one-time seller application, including a store name and payout bank account.
  • You're responsible for keeping your account credentials and session secure.

8. Changes to these terms

Amana may update these terms from time to time as the marketplace evolves. Continued use of Amana after a change means you accept the updated terms. Material changes will be reflected in the "last updated" date at the top of this page.

9. Contact

Questions about these terms, a specific order, or a dispute can be sent to Amana directly via WhatsApp from the main site.
